ISLA COZUMEL

The largest island in the Mexican Caribbean—and third-largest in the country—Isla Cozumel sits off the tip of the peninsula, near Playa del Carmen.
Measuring about 30 miles long and 9 miles wide, Isla Cozumel, whose Mayan name translates to “Island of the Swallows”—a nod to its indigenous bird population—has become a cruise ship favorite, as well as a hotspot for divers, since It is one of the best dive sites in the world, with reef walls plunging to incredible depths.
